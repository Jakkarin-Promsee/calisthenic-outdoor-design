> **Key limitation of this document** — This agreement is intended to allocate and limit civil liability between Party A and Party B. An agreement between parties cannot exclude criminal liability or liability to third parties (e.g., injured users or their families). Genuine protection comes from: (1) ensuring actual conduct matches the roles stated on paper, and not taking on responsibilities beyond one's competence; (2) having a licensed engineer certify and take responsibility for the design; and (3) working carefully and maintaining complete documentary evidence.

---

## 1. Purpose

This project involves four parties (see §2). This document is intended to **clearly define the scope, role boundaries, and liability allocation between Party A (Researcher) and Party B (Coordinator)**, specifically:

1. To confirm that **Party A and Party B are researchers and coordinators only** — not the construction contractor, not the certifying engineer, and not the decision-maker for approving construction.
2. To establish clearly that **structural integrity, engineering safety, welding, foundations, installation, and the decision to approve construction** are the roles and responsibilities of **Party C (Owner/Licensed Engineer)** and **Party D (Contractor)**.
3. To define the "handoff" mechanism from Party A → Party B → Party C, so that it is clear at which point each party's responsibilities end and are transferred.

The document used to communicate and record the handover of roles with Party C is kept separately in [policy.handoff.md](policy.handoff.md) — kept concise and clear so as not to confuse the owner with this internal agreement.

In this project, Party C is **a single individual** filling three roles simultaneously: owner, licensed engineer, and site inspector. The requirement for "a licensed engineer to certify" is therefore fulfilled by Party C themselves.

## 7. Handoff Mechanism

To make clear **at what point each party's responsibilities end and transfer**:

1. **Party A → Party B:** Party A's work is "preliminary draft inputs" that have **no effect** until Party B reviews and decides to **adopt, modify, or pass on** the information. Once Party B adopts/modifies/passes on the information, Party B has exercised their own judgement, and the responsibility for "choosing to use" that information belongs to Party B.
2. **Party B → Party C:** When Party B delivers the design package to Party C under [policy.handoff.md](policy.handoff.md), **responsibility for review, verification, certification, approval, and the construction decision** passes to Party C as the licensed engineer and owner.
3. **Party C → Party D:** Party C hires and supervises Party D to build per the approved design. Construction quality is the joint responsibility of Party C (controls) and Party D (performs).

**Effect on Party A:** Party A is not responsible for (a) Party B's choice to adopt or modify data; (b) the decision to proceed with construction; (c) Party C's certification/approval; (d) Party D's workmanship; or (e) on-site conditions.

**Effect on Party B:** Party B is not responsible for (a) engineering certification/approval; (b) actual construction quality; or (c) the decision to approve construction, which belongs to Party C.

## 8. Liability Disclaimer

1. The designs and assessments prepared by Parties A and B are **functional design and preliminary assessment compiled from publicly available standards and manufacturer data** — not structural calculations signed off by a licensed engineer.
2. Parties A and B **do not warrant** structural strength, safety, or fitness for purpose of the equipment.
3. Parties A and B are **not liable** for damages, injuries, or losses arising from construction, structural failure, welding, foundations, installation, material quality, workmanship quality, or use beyond the design intent.
4. If the design or materials are changed from what is specified, Party A is not responsible for the consequences; all changes must be reviewed and approved by Party C.
5. **Nature of the accompanying research data:** The reference calculations, standards, and material data compiled by Parties A and B and **delivered with the design package** are **compiled, quoted, or referenced from publicly available internet sources** (standards documents, manufacturer websites, technical articles, etc.) — Parties A and B are **not the authors of those standards or the original calculators** and **make no warranty of accuracy, completeness, currency, or fitness for this specific application.** All data are delivered "as-is" for Party C to independently verify before use.

## 9. "Recommend, Not Must" Principle

1. Parties A and B **strongly recommend** that Party C review, compute/confirm, and certify the design — including inspecting welds, foundations, and concrete condition — before opening for use.
2. **The level and method of verification, as well as approval for construction and opening for use, are the professional judgement and responsibility of Party C** as the licensed engineer and owner. Parties A and B have no authority and no duty to make these decisions for Party C.
3. **For genuine safety**, Parties A and B recommend that structural strength be verified and certified before opening for use, especially **before allowing children to use the equipment**, and that fall protection surfaces (e.g., EPDM rubber) be installed as specified in the design.

## 10. Working Policy (A ↔ B)

1. Parties A and B **will not sign as "construction contractor" or "certifying engineer"** and will sign only as "Researcher" and "Coordinator" respectively.
2. Party B will deliver the design package to Party C together with the [Design Handover Letter](policy.handoff.md) and will retain a signed copy as evidence.
3. Both parties will **keep work records** (draft drawings, research data, communications, handover records) as evidence that work was done with care and that certification was recommended.
4. Roles are separated in writing, signed by both parties, and a copy is retained by each party.

---

## 11. Critical Condition — Actual Conduct Must Match Stated Roles

Protection under this document is effective only when actual conduct matches the stated roles.

1. If in practice Party B **directs, controls, or personally performs construction/welding/installation** in the manner of a contractor, a court or opposing party may deem Party B to be the actual contractor regardless of what the document says.
2. If Party C is **not actually a licensed engineer** or does not actually hire/supervise the contractor, this role-separation structure will provide significantly less protection.
3. Therefore all parties should **act in accordance with their stated roles** and maintain supporting evidence (e.g., the construction contract in Party C's name; design certification by Party C).

## 12. Applicable Thai Law (Reference Only)

The following laws are relevant background — the parties acknowledge them:

| Law | Relevance (summary) |
|---|---|
| **Engineering Profession Act B.E. 2542 (1999)** | Structural design/calculation work may constitute "controlled engineering work" requiring a licence from the Council of Engineers — reason why Parties A/B must not present themselves as certifiers, and why Party C must certify |
| **Building Control Act B.E. 2522 (1979)** | Certain structures may require a permit and licensed engineer's signature — check with local authority whether this project falls within scope |
| **Criminal Code §291 / §300** | Negligence causing death / serious bodily harm carries criminal penalties — **criminal liability cannot be excluded by private contract** |
| **Civil and Commercial Code §420 (tort)** | Intentional or negligent act causing damage to another → obligation to compensate |
| **CCC §425–428 (employer/principal liability)** | The employer-employee / principal-agent relationship affects liability allocation — reason why the construction contract should be in Party C's name |

Criminal liability and liability to injured users generally cannot be excluded by private agreement. Genuine risk reduction comes from not taking on roles beyond one's competence, working carefully, and having a licensed engineer actually certify the work.
